Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"Missile Man of India"

Introduction

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He is widely admired for his humility, scientific achievements, and inspirational leadership.

Early Life

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Kalam came from a modest background. He sold newspapers to support his family during childhood and was always passionate about learning, science, and innovation.

Achievements

  • Chief architect of India's missile programs (AGNI & PRITHVI).
  • Played a key role in Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Served as President of India (2002-2007).
  • Received Bharat Ratna, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Vibhushan.
  • Inspired millions of students and youth across India.

Famous Quote

“Dream is not that which you see while sleeping, it is something that does not let you sleep.”

Legacy

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture to students. He left behind a powerful legacy of hard work, vision, and selfless service to the nation.
